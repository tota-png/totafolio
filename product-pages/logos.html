Logos

Although I would not call myself a logo designer, I have made some logomarks for different organizations. The most significant collaboration was with UF's Chemistry Department, in the making of a brand identity for their yearly event: the Chemathon.

The inspiration for the Chemathon logo came from 60's cartoons and overall midcentury style. The logo was printed out on shirts and used in all promotional materials for the yearly event organized by UF's Chemistry Department, which hosts high schools from around Florida for a day of science-themed competition.

The Xie Lab at the University of Florida studies microRNAs (miRNAs), short sequences known to interact with messenger RNAs. The logo, used in the lab's Instagram profile (@xielabuf) shows the graphical depiction of a miRNA against the official UF gradient.

The Florida Improv Fest (FIF) is a yearly gathering of Florida's collegiate improv teams for a weekend of workshops and improvisational comedy. UF was the host for the 2025 event, and I was tasked with creating a logo for the event. The logo was inspired by fruit stickers, of which there are many in Florida. Upon request from event organizers, a 3D version of the logo was designed using Blender 3D. This 3D version was used as the official logo for FIF.

The FLACS logo was designed as an exercise to practice making more minimalist logos. It was intended to be a logo for the Florida Local Section of the American Chemical Society.
